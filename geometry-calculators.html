⭕ Circle Calculator

Circle Area & Circumference

What it calculates: Basic circle properties including area and circumference. Enter either radius or diameter.

Formulas: Area = πr², Circumference = 2πr = πd

Circle Sector & Arc Length

What it calculates: A sector is a "pie slice" of a circle, and an arc is the curved edge of that slice.

Sector Area: The area of the pie slice = (θ/360°) × πr²

Arc Length: The length of the curved edge = (θ/360°) × 2πr

Example: A 90° sector of a circle with radius 5 has area = (90/360) × π × 5² = 19.63 square units

Circle Segment Area

What it calculates: A segment is the area between a chord (straight line across the circle) and the arc.

Formula: Segment Area = r² × arccos((r-h)/r) - (r-h) × √(2rh-h²)

Where: r = radius, h = segment height (distance from chord to arc)

Example: A segment with radius 10 and height 3 has area ≈ 28.27 square units
